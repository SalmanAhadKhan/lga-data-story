Is an AirTrain to LGA really a necessary $2.1 billion expense?

As Governor Hochul puts Cuomo's AirTrain on pause, Port Authority figures show a radical difference in number of commuters who would benefit from an LGA AirTrain versus those who currently use the JFK AirTrain.

By Salman Ahad

November 12, 2021

Abandoned LGA signage near a residential property in Queens. Credit: Salman Ahad Khan.

Earlier this year in July, the Federal Aviation Administration approved the construction of an AirTrain that would take passengers from LaGuardia Airport to Manhattan. It was a project first initiated by former Governor Andrew Cuomo and approved by the state legislature in 2018, but Governor Hochul paused the project in October this year. Critics call it a Cuomo vanity project and a waste of money because of how few people would actually benefit from the $2.1 billion project. They say the money is better spent helping Queens residents recover from disasters like Hurricane Ida instead.

According to ‘A Better Way to LGA,’ a coalition of several local groups, critics misrepresent the project by saying funding should be used for other local needs, since the funds can only be for transportation-related projects. They also argue that it is the only alternative that could be built without disrupting residential or commercial neighborhoods.
